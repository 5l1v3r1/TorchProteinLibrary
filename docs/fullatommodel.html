FullAtomModel

This module contains layers to convert angles to coordinates, load PDB files, assign atom types and transform coordinates of a protein.

class FullAtomModel.Angles2Coords

This layer converts angles and sequences to atomic coordinates. It does so by building a graph of transforms, acting on rigid groups of atoms. For example, Figure 1 shows alanine, it has 5 rigid groups in total and is parametrized by 4 transforms. Each transform is parametrized by one angle and has the following form: $$ R(\alpha, \beta, d) = R_y (\beta) \cdot T_x(d) \cdot R_x(\alpha) $$ where $R_x$ and $R_y$ are the 4x4 rotation matrixes around axis $x$ and $y$; $T_x(d)$ is the 4x4 translation matrix along the axis $x$. The parametrization shown on Figure 1 can be written as follows: $$ R_1(\phi) = R(\phi, \theta_1, d_1)$$ where $\theta_1$ and $d_1$ are fixed parameters of bonds. We do not compute derivatives of these parameters. To compute coordinates of a rigid group of atoms, we need to multiply each coordinate in an intial reference frame by this matrix. During the forward pass we construct a graph of cummulative transformations, shown on the Figure 2. $$\begin{eqnarray} M_1 &=& R_1(\phi)\\ M_2 &=& R_1(\phi) \cdot R_2(\xi)\\ M_3 &=& R_1(\phi) \cdot R_3(\psi)\\ M_4 &=& R_1(\phi) \cdot R_3(\psi) \cdot R_4(\omega) \end{eqnarray}$$ or in shorter form to get the cummulative transform of a node $M_{node}$, we take the cummulative transform $M_{parent~~node}$ and multiply it by the tranform of the current node $R_{node} (parameters)$. The coordinates of atoms are then computed by multiplying each ridig group atomic coordinates by its cummulative transform.

To compute gradients we save matrixes $F_i = M_{i-1} \cdot R_i \cdot M^{-1}_{i}$. The gradients are then computed in reversed deepth-first traversal of the graph: $$ \frac{\partial r_{i}}{\partial \theta_j} += \left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial r_{i}}, \sum_j F_i \cdot r_j \right) $$

Each angle-set for an amino-acid consists of ${\phi, \psi, \xi_1, \xi2, \xi_3, \xi_4, \xi_5}$ in the order of increasing the index. If an amino-acid does no require certain angles for its parametrization, they are ignored. Names are ancoded in ASCII, with the last symbol always set to '\0'.

class FullAtomModel.Coords2TypedCoords

This layer assigns atom types and rearranges coordinates according to types.

class FullAtomModel.PDB2CoordsUnordered

Reads PDB and outputs coordinates, residue and atom names and number of atoms. The order of atoms is the same as in the PDB file.

class FullAtomModel.CoordsTranslate

Translates coordinates.

class FullAtomModel.CoordsRotate

Rotates coordinates.
